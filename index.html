The sequence

Scroll through the chapters.

The visuals and copy are designed like a film sequence. Each chapter changes the atmosphere, the color, and the message instead of just stacking another block of text.

Scene 01

From school structure to a study story.

The platform is built around schools, categories, courses, chapters, and learning content. The landing page should feel like the opening shot of a serious product: clear, polished, and clearly educational.

  • Structured content hierarchy
  • Course and chapter navigation
  • Notes, questions, and exams

Scene 02

The lesson expands when the student enters a chapter.

Video, notes, practice questions, and exams do not sit as separate modules here. They feel like one flow: watch, read, answer, remember, and continue.

  • Video playback with progress continuity
  • Rich notes and offline comfort
  • Timed exams and results

Scene 03

The lesson can continue on TV without losing its place.

The TV app is the living-room branch of the same system. It should feel like a continuation of the lesson, not a separate product.

  • Pairing and remote-first control
  • Large-screen viewing flow
  • Simple navigation for TV devices

Scene 04

The admin layer keeps the system governed.

Teachers, admins, and operators need clarity, not clutter. The product needs to communicate trust, control, and operational calm.

  • Role-based access and dashboards
  • Payments, subscriptions, and review flow
  • Support, logs, and moderation

Scene 05

Now the visitor can leave with the right app.
